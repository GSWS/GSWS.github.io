Research Areas

  • My research focuses on the development of deep learning algorithms, with an emphasis on their application to human-centric AI. Specifically, I concentrate on Representation Learning for Gigapixel Images and Multimodal Learning in the field of Healthcare.
  • Representation Learning for Gigapixel Images: Gigapixel whole-slide imaging (WSI) in computational pathology presents a unique and complex challenge, rendering traditional convolutional neural network based supervised learning approaches impractical. To address this complexity, my research has focused on interpreting large gigapixel images as a graph of image patches from WSI. I have further developed Transformer-based methodologies for both weakly-supervised and self-supervised learning in the context of WSIs.
  • Multimodal Learning: Large volumes of multimodal data are often collected concurrently to investigate the same underlying disease. My research has focused on multimodal learning, specifically integrating pathology images and genomic data to enhance cancer prognosis.
  • Currently I'm focusing my efforts at Biometrics AI in improving the security and accessibility of healthcare data, enabling healthcare providers to deliver more personalized care while ensuring highly accurate patient identification.
